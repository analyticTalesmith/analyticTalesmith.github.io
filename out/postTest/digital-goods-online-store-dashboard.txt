<ul class="">
<li class=""><strong>Source Data&nbsp;</strong>— A four-table data set was generated <a href="https://analytictalesmith.com/digital-goods-data-simulation/" title="">using Python</a> to simulate sales from an online digital goods store for the sake of this practice case study.</li>



<li class=""><strong>Problem&nbsp;</strong>— How well is the shop performing, what items sell best, and what items can be paired together in promotions to boost sales?</li>



<li class=""><strong>Insights&nbsp;</strong>—
<ul class="">
<li class="">More users are ordering digital downloads from the site, with the overall number of orders trending upward.</li>



<li class="">The digital assets created without a specific tool/program in mind sell really well, though the average profit per general purpose asset sold is lower than other categories.</li>



<li class="">Templates and assets made for Slides, Powerpoint, and Notion sell the best, with Notion templates selling the second most assets by category and Slides assets generating the second highest profit by category</li>



<li class="">Though assets for Word, Excel, Docs, and Canva sell similarly to other categories, the profit they generate lags behind</li>
</ul>
</li>



<li class=""><strong>Recommendations </strong>—
<ul class="">
<li class=""><em>Design Bundles or Promos from Clusters</em> — If your users are already buying certain items together, then take advantage of this knowledge and try to drive additional sales by rewarding customers with discounts for buying them together.</li>



<li class=""><em>Assess General Purpose Asset Prices </em>— The gap between units sold and profit in the general purpose category indicates that prices might be too low for the category. However, this is not <em>necessarily</em> the case, and they may simply be selling well because they&#8217;re what the store is known for.</li>



<li class=""><em>Identify Any Changes December &#8217;22 &#8211; Jan &#8217;23</em> — The number of orders jumped significantly in Jan. 2023, and figuring out whether this was noise or a result of something the store did (promotion, new products, etc.) would be beneficial for developing product development and marketing strategies moving forward. </li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
